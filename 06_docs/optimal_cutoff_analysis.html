Policy Context — Why the Existing 14% Hurdle Differs from the Simulation Optimum

What the 14% Hurdle Represents

The 14% hurdle reflects a policy choice based on historical capital allocation objectives — cost of capital, target ROE, strategic risk appetite, and regulatory capital constraints. It is not mathematically incorrect; it embeds a conservative premium above breakeven to maintain a buffer against estimation error, macro deterioration, and concentration risk. It is an economically and strategically chosen threshold.

What the Simulation Finds

Under the current modelling assumptions (LGD 0.45, cost of capital 17%, Basel risk weights, product-rate revenue), the efficient frontier for portfolio net income peaks at 17–18% approval. The legacy hurdle, calibrated to a different objective function (RAROC maximisation with capital buffer), produces 12.2% approval — the same portfolio at lower NI and higher capital efficiency. Both are valid depending on the committee's stated objective.

The Analytical Recommendation

Under the stated objective — maximise portfolio net income with every approved loan carrying positive RAROC — the frontier peaks at ≈17% approval. This is presented as the Portfolio Economic Optimum (Current Assumptions), not as a definitive replacement for institution-level capital policy. Committees optimising for different objectives may rationally select a different point on the frontier.

Robustness

Net income at the ≈17% approval gate remains positive across all LGD assumptions from 30% to 60%. At LGD=60%, portfolio NI is still +₹46M and RAROC 5.3%. The direction of the recommendation is robust to LGD estimation error.

Simulation Optimum Shifts with LGD

LGD = 35%: frontier peaks at ~29% approval (+₹325M NI)

LGD = 45%: frontier peaks at ~17–18% approval (+₹183M NI)

LGD = 55%: frontier peaks at ~12% approval (+₹110M NI)

The conservative 14% hurdle (producing ~12% approval) is implicitly consistent with a pessimistic LGD assumption of ~55%. This is one valid interpretation of why the legacy hurdle was set where it was.

Scope & Objective Function

Under the current assumptions and objective function — portfolio net income maximisation subject to positive loan-level RAROC, LGD 0.45, cost of capital 17% — the efficient frontier peaks at approximately 17–18% approval. This is the Portfolio Economic Optimum (Current Assumptions), not a universal optimum. The legacy 14% RAROC hurdle reflects a different, equally valid objective: RAROC maximisation with a conservative buffer above estimated cost of capital, implicitly consistent with a more pessimistic LGD assumption. Committees optimising for shareholder return targets, concentration limits, regulatory capital ratios, or multi-period growth objectives may rationally select a different point on the frontier. The analysis quantifies the tradeoffs; the policy choice remains with the committee.

ECL = EAD × PD × LGD. Economic Capital = Basel risk-weight formula scaled by LGD. LGD sensitivity: gross income held constant, ECL and capital scaled linearly with LGD ratio. Simulation Baseline = all 61,503 applicants approved, used as a computational reference for the value of risk-based selection — not an industry benchmark. Approval rate at each threshold is a consequence of applying that RAROC gate to this specific portfolio; rates will differ on other portfolios.
