01 WHAT THIS IS
“

Most credit risk projects train a model and show an AUC score. This one trains five models across three real datasets, chains their outputs through a weighted signal aggregator, applies a five-layer decision framework, generates regulatory-compliant adverse action notices, logs every decision with a full SR 11-7 audit trail, and monitors model drift against live GREEN/AMBER/RED thresholds. It is a system, not a model.

The system replicates the credit origination workflow of a regulated Indian lender end-to-end. Each module answers a distinct risk question, the Decision Engine combines all three signals into a single explainable outcome, and Governance closes the loop with model cards, regulatory alignment, and automated monitoring.

11 BUSINESS IMPACT   CHAMPION VS CHALLENGER

THE CEO DOES NOT CARE ABOUT AUC.

You improved Gini. You improved KS. You improved OOT performance. Great. But business promotes models because they deliver better outcomes — not because they score higher on internal metrics. In credit risk, the real question is: did your Challenger beat the Champion on the three success criteria a credit committee actually uses?

#1
Same Approval Rate
Hold the approval rate constant at the Champion level. Did the new model reduce the default rate in the approved population? Same volume. Safer book. The lender wins.
#2
Same Default Rate
Hold the default rate constant at the Champion level. Did the new model approve more good loans? Same risk. More revenue. The business wins.
GOLD STANDARD
#3
Both Move Favourably
Approval rate increases AND default rate decreases. This is where real business value is created. This is what gets a model into production.

The strategy simulator (NB05) already computed approval rates, default rates, and RAROC for all four strategies. NB07 formalises these as a structured Champion vs Challenger evaluation. The table below is the output. Reference: Aggressive strategy as the baseline (the simplest deployable model).

"I improved AUC from 0.74 to 0.76 and KS from 0.36 to 0.38."
"Gini, KS, and PSI were the foundation — they confirmed the model discriminates and is stable. But we evaluated production-readiness on business criteria. Versus the Aggressive baseline at 44.7% approval rate, the Risk-Based Pricing champion lifted RAROC from -6.73% to +47.31% with identical volume. Under a fixed approval rate constraint, RAROC Gated reduced the default rate in the approved book from 2.70% to 1.27%. Both are the success criteria credit committees use to approve model deployment."
