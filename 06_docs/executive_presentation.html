Why 17% not 12%?

Approximately 3,076 economically profitable loans (RAROC ≥ 2.5%) are excluded by the legacy 14% hurdle. Including them adds +₹18M NI at only marginal ECL cost. The 14% hurdle is consistent with an implicit LGD assumption of ~55% — valid for a pessimistic scenario but leaving value on the table at LGD 0.45.

LGD Robustness

Net income at the ≈17% gate remains positive across all LGD scenarios from 30% to 60%. At LGD=60% (severe stress), NI is still +₹46M and RAROC 5.3%. The recommendation is directionally robust to LGD estimation error.

Vintage Analysis — Key Findings

Portfolio quality drift: Default rate rose from 4.6% (2016 cohort) to 9.7% (2026 cohort) — recent originations are ~2× riskier than older cohorts
Peak loss period: First 6 months (DR 9.4–10.0%) — early seasoning hump typical of retail portfolios; underwriting discipline at origination is critical
Survival at 36M: 57.3% of loans survive the full 3-year cycle without default
PD model calibration: Model overpredicts default at all bands (ratio 0.14×–0.23×) — conservative, appropriate for regulatory use
ECL coverage: Provision broadly adequate; 2024–2026 vintages may warrant quota review given rising trend
